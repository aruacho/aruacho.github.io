Projects

Airport Departure Management

I developed a simulation to assess the need for an additional runway at an airport. The focus of the simulation is on planes departing the airport, utilizing a queue-based approach to manage their departure. Each plane is represented by a class object with attributes such as ID, initial fuel, and departure queue time. The simulation considers factors like the total number of planes, minimum fuel needed for flight, simulation time, departing queue time, and initial fuel for planes. The program creates a realistic scenario by decrementing fuel for each plane in the simulation and processing the departure queue based on specified conditions. The main method initializes the simulation, runs a loop, and prints insightful information, such as the number of planes that successfully departed, planes with insufficient fuel, and those still on the runway. The flexibility to adjust simulation parameters allows for dynamic exploration of the effects on plane departures.
